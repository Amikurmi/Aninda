Major Contributions

Comprehensive Biography and Professional Timeline

Dr. Aninda Sidhana’s career is rooted in a deep personal and familial connection to mental health. Born into a family of psychiatrists, she was exposed early to the complexities of psychological well-being and the nuances of care. Her academic journey began with an MBBS from Padmashree Dr. D. Y. Patil Medical College, followed by an MD in Psychiatry from Sri Balajai Vidyapeeth Mahatma Gandhi Medical College, Puducherry. She further specialized with a Fellowship in Psychosexual Medicine from JSS Medical College, Mysore.

Her professional trajectory spans over a decade, encompassing roles in general hospital psychiatry, tertiary neuropsychiatry centers, and academic institutions. Notably, she has served as:

  • Assistant Professor at Dr. S.S. Tantia M.C.H. & Research Centre (2024-present)
  • Guest Faculty for the Fellowship & Certificate Course in Psychosexual Medicine at JSS Medical College, Mysore (2024-present)
  • Consultant Psychiatrist at Tekchand Sidana Memorial Hospital (2024-present)
  • Assistant Professor at Santosh University, Ghaziabad (2017-2018)
  • Senior Resident at the Institute of Human Behaviour and Allied Sciences (IHBAS), Delhi (2013-2016)

Her clinical expertise encompasses stress management, addictions, marital therapy, and the management of severe and chronic mental illnesses. Dr. Sidhana’s unique focus on women’s mental and sexual health, informed by both clinical training and lived experience, has positioned her as a leading voice in breaking taboos and advancing inclusive care.

Key Initiatives and Contributions

The Dignity Dialogues

Founded in 2025, The Dignity Dialogues is a survivor-led initiative that reimagines mental health and gender justice through poetic storytelling, trauma-integrated care, and media collaborations. The program centers lived experience, building healing systems rooted in dignity, agency, and collective transformation.

Its core components include:

  • Healing Justice Toolkit: Co-created with survivors, this resource provides trauma-integrated care strategies for individuals, educators, and policymakers.
  • Listening Circles: Facilitated across Rajasthan, these circles gather survivor insights to inform systemic change and foster community healing.
  • Media Collaborations: Partnerships with platforms like The Better India and AboutHer Show ensure ethical representation and amplify survivor voices.
  • Impact Dashboard: Tracks emotional resonance, reach, and survivor-led transformation, institutionalizing dignity and agency as pillars of gender justice.

The Dignity Dialogues has directly impacted over 500 survivors through listening circles and toolkit access, and more than 200 mental health professionals via workshops. Indirectly, its digital campaigns and coalition networks have reached thousands across India and globally.

Aawaz: Safe Haven for Survivors

Aawaz, meaning “voice,” is Dr. Sidhana’s forthcoming initiative designed as a sanctuary for survivors of GBV. It will offer counseling, support groups, and advocacy programs, aiming to provide holistic care and empower survivors to reclaim their narratives. The program’s design is informed by Dr. Sidhana’s personal journey and her commitment to breaking the silence around trauma and recovery.

Invisible Scars Foundation

As a psychiatrist with the Invisible Scars Foundation, Dr. Sidhana has contributed to the development of trauma-informed care models, survivor support systems, and public education campaigns. The foundation’s work integrates clinical expertise with advocacy, focusing on the invisible wounds of emotional and psychological abuse.

Clinical Practice and Psychosexual Medicine

Dr. Sidhana’s clinical work is distinguished by her specialization in psychosexual medicine—a field often neglected in mainstream mental health discourse. She addresses issues such as sexual dissatisfaction, trauma, and shame, particularly among women, advocating for integrated treatment that encompasses both physical and emotional health. Her practice emphasizes the inseparability of sexual and mental wellness, challenging societal taboos and promoting comprehensive sex education for women.

Academic Roles and Teaching Contributions

As Assistant Professor at Dr. S.S. Tantia M.C.H. & Research Centre and Guest Faculty at JSS Medical College, Dr. Sidhana has shaped the next generation of mental health professionals. She mentors students in psychosexual medicine, delivers lectures on trauma-informed care, and develops curricula that integrate clinical rigor with empathy and inclusivity. Her teaching philosophy centers on empowering students to address the unique realities of women’s mental health, GBV, and sexual wellness.
