Comprehensive Biography & Professional Timeline

Dr. Aninda Sidhana's career is rooted in a deep personal and familial connection to mental health. Born into a family of psychiatrists, she was exposed early to the complexities of psychological well-being and the nuances of care. Her academic journey began with an MBBS from Padmashree Dr. D. Y. Patil Medical College, followed by an MD in Psychiatry from Sri Balaji Vidyapeeth Mahatma Gandhi Medical College, Puducherry, and a Fellowship in Psychosexual Medicine from JSS Medical College, Mysore.

Her professional trajectory spans over a decade, encompassing roles in general hospital psychiatry, tertiary neuropsychiatric centers, and academic institutions:

  • Assistant Professor at Dr. S.S. Tantia M.C.H. & Research Centre (2024–present)
  • Guest Faculty for the Fellowship & Certificate Course in Psychosexual Medicine at JSS Medical College, Mysore (2024–present)
  • Consultant Psychiatrist at Tekchand Sidana Memorial Hospital (2024–present)
  • Assistant Professor at Santosh University, Ghaziabad (2017–2018)
  • Senior Resident at the Institute of Human Behaviour and Allied Sciences (IHBAS), Delhi (2013–2016)

Her clinical expertise encompasses stress management, addictions, marital therapy, and the management of severe and chronic mental illnesses. Her unique focus on women's mental and sexual health—informed by both clinical training and lived experience—has positioned her as a leading voice in breaking taboos and advancing inclusive care.

Key Initiatives & Contributions

Founded 2025
The Dignity Dialogues

A survivor-led initiative that reimagines mental health and gender justice through poetic storytelling, trauma-integrated care, and media collaborations. The program centers lived experience, building healing systems rooted in dignity, agency, and collective transformation.

  • 01
    Healing Justice Toolkit: Co-created with survivors, providing trauma-integrated care strategies for individuals, educators, and policymakers.
  • 02
    Listening Circles: Facilitated across Rajasthan, gathering survivor insights to inform systemic change and foster community healing.
  • 03
    Media Collaborations: Partnerships with The Better India and AboutHer Show, ensuring ethical representation and amplifying survivor voices.
  • 04
    Impact Dashboard: Tracks emotional resonance, reach, and survivor-led transformation, institutionalizing dignity and agency as pillars of gender justice.

The Dignity Dialogues has directly impacted over 500 survivors through listening circles and toolkit access, and more than 200 mental health professionals via workshops. Its digital campaigns and coalition networks have reached thousands across India and globally.

Forthcoming Initiative
Aawaz: Safe Haven for Survivors

Aawaz, meaning "voice," is designed as a sanctuary for survivors of gender-based violence. It will offer counseling, support groups, and advocacy programs, providing holistic care and empowering survivors to reclaim their narratives. The program's design is informed by Dr. Sidhana's personal journey and her commitment to breaking the silence around trauma and recovery.

Collaborative Role
Invisible Scars Foundation

As a psychiatrist with the Invisible Scars Foundation, Dr. Sidhana has contributed to the development of trauma-informed care models, survivor support systems, and public education campaigns. The foundation's work integrates clinical expertise with advocacy, focusing on the invisible wounds of emotional and psychological abuse.

Clinical Specialization
Clinical Practice & Psychosexual Medicine

Dr. Sidhana's clinical work is distinguished by her specialization in psychosexual medicine—a field often neglected in mainstream mental health discourse. She addresses sexual dissatisfaction, trauma, and shame, particularly among women, advocating for integrated treatment encompassing both physical and emotional health. Her practice challenges societal taboos and promotes comprehensive sexual wellness education.

Academic Leadership
Academic Roles & Teaching Contributions

As Assistant Professor at Dr. S.S. Tantia M.C.H. & Research Centre and Guest Faculty at JSS Medical College, Dr. Sidhana shapes the next generation of mental health professionals. She mentors students in psychosexual medicine, delivers lectures on trauma-informed care, and develops curricula that integrate clinical rigor with empathy and inclusivity. Her teaching centers on empowering students to address women's mental health, GBV, and sexual wellness.
